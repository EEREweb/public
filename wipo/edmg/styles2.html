Define the Merits of Tracking Energy Data

Organizations that have established robust energy data tracking systems report benefits in three areas: energy and cost savings, the ability to set realistic energy reduction goals, and improved control of energy budgets.

Energy and Cost Savings

  • Energy savings averaging 2 percent annually
  • Data-driven decision-making for improved energy efficiency program management
  • Enhanced efficiencies in business operations
  • Design and verification of energy efficiency projects and programs

Goal Setting

  • Realistic energy reduction targets based on measured baselines
  • Tracking progress towards energy and greenhouse gas reduction goals
  • Recognition for high performance
  • Culture of continuous improvement and accountability

Control of Energy Budgets

  • Savings for utility tariffs audits
  • Recovery of costs from billing errors
  • Participation in demand response programs
  • Energy purchasing (in deregulated markets)| of Energy Budgets

Local Government Spotlight:
City of Virginia Beach, Virginia
A Collaborative Approach to the Development of an Energy Saving Strategy

In response to rising utility expenditures, the City of Virginia Beach established a Joint Energy Committee (JEC) in 2008 to identify, assess, and implement energy-related activities. The JEC is chaired by the Deputy City Manager, and comprises representatives from city departments, the City Public Schools, and the city's major electric utility, Dominion Virginia Power.
